Quadcopter Control

The goal of this work is PoC for methodology and software to calculate adaptive close-loop control for a quadcopter. We are talking about smart auto-pilot which allows the UAV to flight autonomously, ideally without human operator.

Quadcopter is described by simplified (air resistance during movement and wind are neglected) non-linear dynamic model with dimensions of state and control vectors are 12 and 4 respectively. Iterative linear quadratic regulator is used for control. For each maneuver we would set up desirable trajectories of state coordinates and calculate feedbacks vs. time to minimize deviation between actual and desirable trajectories.

Quadcopter is highly non-linear, non-stable and considerably underactuated dynamic system. These features are taken into account during control generation.

Some results are presented below. The plots depict transients (translations and Euler angles vs. time) of quadcopter for different maneuvers performed under proposed control algorithms.

1. Transition from hover point with all zero initial conditions to a hover point with x = 10 m, y = -1 m, z = 1 m.

2. Movement from hover point in horizontal plane with constant speeds dx/dt = 2 m/s and dy/dt = -3 m/s.

3. Brake from horizontal movement with constant speeds dx/dt = 2 m/s and dy/dt = -3 m/s to hover.
